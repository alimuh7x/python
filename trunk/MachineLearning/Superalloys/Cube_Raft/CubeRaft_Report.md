# Cube-to-Raft Transition Analysis

## Overview

Morphological analysis of the cube-to-raft transition in $\gamma'$ precipitates using `fast_rs` for Wadell sphericity and roundness. Three phase-field datasets (initial, 0.6% strain, 1.0% strain) are compared on a single $z=40$ slice from $128^3$ grids with $dx = 30$ nm.

## Morphology and Shape Plots

![Morphology comparison](comparison_morphology.png){ align=right width=45% }

Volume, diameter, aspect ratio, sphericity, roundness, and nearest-neighbor histograms (unnormalized on the left, normalized on the right).



Scatter of sphericity vs roundness for all stages (left: counts, right: normalized point size).

**Trend highlights (independent of histogram shapes)**

- Sphericity drops steadily (0.96 → 0.93 → 0.79) while roundness stays high (0.97 → 0.96 → 0.92), indicating smoother but more anisotropic rafts.
- Aspect ratio climbs (1.12 → 1.42 → 2.35), confirming elongation/rafting despite similar number density spacing.
- Mean spacing rises modestly (461 → 474 → 493 nm), reflecting lower areal density even as 3D volume fraction stays >0.58.

## Key Observations

- Progressive decrease in $\gamma'$ area fraction and number density from initial to 1.0% strain.
- Size growth with strain: mean volume and equivalent diameter increase; aspect ratio jumps to 2.35 at 1.0% strain (strong elongation/rafting).
- Shape changes: sphericity drops from 0.96 → 0.79; roundness stays high (0.97 → 0.92), indicating smoother but more anisotropic rafts.
- Spacing widens modestly (460 → 493 nm), consistent with coarsening and lower areal density.
